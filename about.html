Before the World Wide Web dominated the internet, Bulletin Board Systems (BBSs) were the primary way for computer enthusiasts to connect and share information. Users would dial in using modems, often waiting for busy signals, to access these digital communities.

BBSs offered message boards, file downloads, and most memorably, "door games" - external programs that provided interactive entertainment. These games were often the main attraction, drawing users back day after day.

ğŸ“ What Were Door Games?

Door games were called "doors" because they were external programs that users could "enter" from the main BBS menu. When a user selected a door game, they would temporarily leave the BBS software and enter the game's interface.

â° Turn-Based Gameplay

Most games limited players to a certain number of "turns" per day, encouraging daily visits.

BBS door games represent a unique period in computing history when online communities were small, personal, and innovative. They pioneered many concepts we take for granted today: persistent online worlds, user-generated content, and social gaming.
